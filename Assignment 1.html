What exactly are Black holes?

Black holes are astronomical objects with gravitational forces so unbelievably strong that Light can't escape them. These Daunting elements of Space are born when a star collapses under its own weight. This cave-in, if you will, would cause a Supernova explosion that would shoot the star's outer layers off. And if the star core had three times the sun's mass, then it has a very high chance of turning into a black hole. Afterward, the black hole starts pulling nearby matter into it, ranging from stars, gas, planets, and even its own kind if they are small enough.
