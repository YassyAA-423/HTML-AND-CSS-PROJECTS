Exploring a Bit more about Black Holes

The following is a video from the National Geographic YouTube channel. It's a short video, but it does offer more information on black holes.

Here's a cool fact A discovery happened in 2015 when scientists first detected gravitational waves, ripples in the fabric of space-time. LIGO detected these waves from an event called GW150914. This event was when two black holes spiraled into each other and joined together over a billion years ago.

Another cool fact is The nearest black hole is Gaia BH1, which is roughly 1,500 light-years away (8,817,938,059,775,412 Miles or 14,191,095,708,871,200 Kilometers).

Here is a quote from Nasa Website:

The supermassive black hole is located at the heart of a galaxy called M87, located about 55 million light-years away, and weighs more than 6 billion solar masses. Its event horizon extends so far it could encompass much of our solar system out to well beyond the planets.
